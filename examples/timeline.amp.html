Motörhead's Lemmy: 1945-2015

  • 24 December 1945

    Ian Fraser "Lemmy" Kilmister is born in Stoke-On-Trent, UK. His nickname reportedly came from his younger wheeling and dealing days, when he would ask people “lemme borrow a fiver”.

    Lemmy played in bands around the north of England as a teenager before moving to London in 1967, where he landed a job as a roadie for The Jimi Hendrix Experience. Hendrix, he later said, was a gentleman because he "always stood up when a woman walked into the room".

  • 1972

    Lemmy joins the band Hawkwind, playing bass guitar and providing vocals on some of their tracks.

  • 1975

    Motörhead - Lemmy Kilmister, Phil "Philthy Animal" Taylor and Eddie Clarke Photo: Rex

    Lemmy is fired from Hawkwind after being arrested on drug charges, and forms a new band in London with drummer Lucas Fox (who is soon replaced by Phil "Philthy Animal" Taylor) and guitarist Larry Wallis (later replaced by Eddie Clarke).

    Originally called “Bastard”, the band decide to change their name to Motörhead, after realising that TV and radio stations might object to their preferred moniker.

  • August 21, 1977

    Motörhead release their first, self-titled album. It's a minor chart success, but defines the group's style.

  • June 27, 1981

    No Sleep 'til Hammersmith, Motörhead’s first live album – and their first UK number one album – is released.

  • August 22, 2005

    Image: Channel 4

    A Channel Four documentary, Motörhead: Live Fast, Die Old, claims that Lemmy has slept with 2,000 women. Lemmy later qualifies the claim, suggesting the true figure is closer to 1,000.

  • November 21, 2013

    Photo: Rex

    Lemmy, who is rumoured to drink a bottle of Jack Daniels Day, reveals that he has cut back on the habit. "I stopped drinking Jack Daniel's and Coke because the sugar in the Coke wasn't good for my diabetes," he tells Classic Rock.

  • August 28, 2015

    Motörhead’s album Bad Magic

    Motörhead’s 22nd studio album – Lemmy’s final album as the band’s frontman – is released, and reaches number 10 in the UK charts.

  • December 26, 2015

    Lemmy in November 2015 Photo: REX/Shutterstock

    Lemmy learns that he has an aggressive form of cancer.

  • December 28, 2015

    Photo: EPA/PETER KLAUNZER

    Two days after his diagnosis, Lemmy dies at home in Los Angeles, at the age of 70.
