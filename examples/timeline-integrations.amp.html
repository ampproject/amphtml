    Ian Fraser "Lemmy" Kilmister is born in Stoke-On-Trent, UK. His nickname reportedly came from his younger wheeling and dealing days, when he would ask people “lemme borrow a fiver”.

    Lemmy played in bands around the north of England as a teenager before moving to London in 1967, where he landed a job as a roadie for The Jimi Hendrix Experience. Hendrix, he later said, was a gentleman because he "always stood up when a woman walked into the room".

  • 1975

    Motörhead - Lemmy Kilmister, Phil "Philthy Animal" Taylor and Eddie Clarke Photo: Rex

    Lemmy is fired from Hawkwind after being arrested on drug charges, and forms a new band in London with drummer Lucas Fox (who is soon replaced by Phil "Philthy Animal" Taylor) and guitarist Larry Wallis (later replaced by Eddie Clarke).

    Originally called “Bastard”, the band decide to change their name to Motörhead, after realising that TV and radio stations might object to their preferred moniker.
