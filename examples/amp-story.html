What's left behind on the Moon?

CORBIS VIA GETTY

The Chinese Chang’e-4 probe is the latest human object to reach the Moon. But among the other probes, rocket parts and landers, what else is littering the lunar surface? Here's a selection.

NASA/NEWSMAKERS/GETTY

Neil Armstrong may have taken one small step, but he left more than footprints. The US flag he planted, and others left by subsequent missions, are expected to be bleached white by now.

NASA/LIAISON/GETTY

A Moon buggy! The Apollo 15 mission in 1971 was the first mission to use a lunar rover to carry astronauts about. The rover was also used by the Apollo 16 and Apollo 17 missions.

CORBIS VIA GETTY

But they weren’t the first to drive... Apollo 14's Alan Shepard hit two golf balls he'd smuggled inside his spacesuit. His colleague Edgar Mitchell threw a lunar scoop as a javelin.

GETTY

Reaching for the Moon was not without its risks. Apollo 15 astronauts left a plaque naming 14 US astronauts and Russian cosmonauts who had died during manned space programmes.

SSPL/GETTY

Astronaut Charles Moss Duke left a photograph of himself, his wife and two sons on the surface of the moon during the Apollo 16 mission.

SPACE FRONTIERS/GETTY

There is also a large amount of photographic equipment, including 12 Hasselblad cameras. One Hasselblad that did make it back to Earth was sold for $835,000 in 2014.

SSPL/GETTY

US geologist Gene Shoemaker devoted his life to studying cosmic impacts. But his lunar dreams were only met when his ashes were slammed into the Moon on board the Lunar Prospector probe.

RESSMEYER/CORBIS/VCG/GETTY

But they are not the only human remains. There are around 96 packets of human excrement, urine and vomit left behind by the 12 astronauts who briefly called the Moon home.
